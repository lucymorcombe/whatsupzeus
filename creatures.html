Centaur

A centaur is a half human, half horse creature. They have the torso and head of a human and the lower body and legs of a horse. In a lot of stories, centaurs are shown to be aggressive drunks, picking fights with each other and any potential threats. Some centaurs, though, were known for their wisdom. The most famous centaur in Greek mythology is Chiron, who was a respected teacher of many Greek heroes.

Pegasus

Pegasus was a beautiful flying horse and was the child of Poseidon and the gorgon Medusa. It was sent to the hero Bellerophon to help him kill the chimera. After this, Pegasus became a messenger for the Gods and was responsible for holding Zeus' thunderbolts.

Cerberus

Cerberus was a three-headed dog who guarded the gates of the Underworld and made sure that those who entered could never leave. For one of his twelve labours, Hercules had to capture Cerberus and, despite getting stung by its snake-like tail, he succeeded.

Sphinx

The sphinx had the face of a woman, the body of a lion and the wings of an eagle. The sphinx was known for terrorising the city of Thebes, asking all who passed her to answer a riddle and killing those who could not answer. The riddle was this:

Which is the creature that has one voice, but has four feet in the morning, two feet in the afternoon, and three feet at night?

The answer was "man", and after Oedipus gave the sphinx the correct answer, she threw herself from a cliff in anger and no longer threatened the people of Thebes.

Hydra

The hydra was a giant snake with nine heads. Many tried to kill the hydra, but this was a difficult task because once one head was cut off, two more would grow in its place. Hercules killed the hydra as one of his twelve labours.

Minotaur

The Minotaur was a flesh-eating monster with the head of a bull and the body of a man. The Minotaur's father was King Minos of Crete, and he was kept in an underground maze beneath the palace. Each year seven boys and seven girls would be chosen to be fed to the Minotaur. This happened until a famous Greek hero named Theseus was chosen as one of the seven boys. He killed the Minotaur and escaped, ending its reign of terror.

Chimera

The chimera was a lion-like creature, with a goat's head coming out of its back and a snake for a tail. It was a fire breathing monster that terrorised the people of Greece. The chimera was killed by the hero Bellerophon, with the help of Pegasus.
