Zeus was secretly raised in Crete by a goat named Amalthea! His father, Kronos, believed that he had eaten his 6th son, just as he had eaten the 5 that came before him. Prophecies told Kronos that he would be overthrown by one of his children, so he believed that the only way to avoid this fate was to swallow his new-borns whole.

By the time Zeus was born, Zeus’ mum Rhea couldn’t watch her husband eat another one of her children. She tricked Kronos by giving him a baby-shaped rock to swallow instead and then snuck off to Crete to give birth to Zeus. And she raised him to take revenge on his dad.

When he turned 17 Zeus’ mum brought him back home. The shock of finding out his son was still alive led Kronos to feel very unwell, so unwell in fact that he vomited. And out of his mouth came the baby-shaped rock that Rhea had fed him 17 years ago – and that was not all!

One by one Kronos vomited up the 5 children he had swallowed. First Hera, then Poseidon, Demeter, Hades and finally Hestia – all fully grown. A 10 year war then started and eventually the other side surrendered, leaving Zeus as King of the Gods.

Zeus decided he wanted to form his own group of leaders, giving each of his siblings a role and sitting them beside him on Olympus. He decided 12 would be the perfect number of Olympians. He and his siblings made 5 as Hades did not count and the remaining spots were filled by some of Zeus’ children.

On Zeus and Hera’s wedding day, they held a competition and challenged their guests to create the best meal they could. The prize for the winner was that they could ask a favour from Zeus.

The winner of this competition was a small creature named Melissa, who had presented the couple with honey, something that Zeus nor Hera had ever tasted before.

Melissa flew up to where Zeus and Hera sat and made her request for a deadly sting. She claimed to need this sting to protect her honey, as it took so long to make and many other creatures tried to steal it.

Zeus was outraged that such an insignificant creature would make such a large request, and he denied her. He only promised that the winner could ask a favour of him – not that it would be granted.

Zeus did, however, create more creatures like Melissa to help her in her work, and she was declared Queen of the colony. He also gave her a sting, but one that would kill her if she ever tried to use it.

This is the story of how bees were created and how honey was discovered.

GOD OF THE SEA

  • ☆ His parents were Kronos and Rhea and he was swallowed whole as a baby.

  • ☆ He was the protector of all waters and could create storms. Sailors would pray to him and sacrifice horses before travelling to ensure they had a safe journey.

  • ☆ He was known for his trident, and he had the power to cause an earthquake by hitting the ground with it.

  • ☆ He had a palace at the bottom of the sea made out of coral.

  • ☆ He created sea nymphs to be the most beautiful creatures in the world and was very angry if anyone claimed to be more beautiful.

Zeus left Poseidon and Hades until last when he was deciding which role each of his siblings would play. The sea and the Underworld would give its ruler huge amounts of power.

Poseidon and Hades hated each other, and both wished for the one that their brother wanted the most.

To make this decision, Zeus held two stones behind his back. A beautiful blue sapphire to represent the sea and a black jet to represent the Underworld.

Poseidon was the first to choose and was given the sapphire, meaning he was the new ruler of the sea, and Hades was the ruler of the Underworld.

Both celebrated, to make their brother jealous. But both wanted what the other had.

GODDESS OF THE HARVEST

  • ☆ Her parents were Kronos and Rhea and she was swallowed whole as a baby.

  • ☆ She and Zeus had one child together, Persephone.

  • ☆ Persephone was taken to the underworld by Hades, but no one knew where she had gone. Demeter grieved and stopped attending to her duties as Goddess of the Harvest. Crops failed and plants were dying. Zeus intervened and they found out where Persephone was. It was agreed that Persephone would spend 6 months of the year in Olympus and the other 6 months in the Underworld with Hades.

☆ According to Greek mythology, Demeter is responsible for the seasons.

☆ For the 6 months that Persephone is gone, Demeter grieves, and the earth is neglected.

☆ This causes autumn and winter.

☆ For the 6 months that Persephone is reunited with her mother, nature thrives through Demeter’s happiness, causing spring and summer.

He owned a three-headed dog named Cerberus who made sure that anyone who entered the Underworld could never leave.

GOD OF MUSIC

  • ☆ His parents were Zeus and Leto, and Artemis was his twin sister.

  • ☆ When Hera, Zeus’ wife, found out that Leto was pregnant with more of Zeus’ children, she caused all land to shun Leto and she had to search far and wide for a place to give birth. Eventually she found a floating island that was not attached to the ocean floor and gave birth there.

  • ☆ He was known for his Lyre, a small musical instrument, and the songs he would play wherever he went. This lyre was made by Hermes and given to Apollo as an apology for stealing one of his cows.

  • ☆ He is often associated with the sun, which was pulled around the world everyday by the sun chariot, led by flying horses. In some mythology Apollo is identified as the driver of this chariot.

  • ☆ He taught medicine to humans and was often called ‘The Healer’.

  • ☆ When he was angry, he would send mice to earth to invade towns and cities, and get rid of them again when he was in a better mood. Because of this, he became known as a ‘Mouse Demon.’

GODDESS OF WISDOM

  • ☆ Her parents were Zeus and Metis.

  • ☆ When Metis was pregnant, Zeus heard a prophecy that she would give birth to a son who would overthrow him. This caused Zeus great concern and he started chasing Metis, trying to kill her and their unborn child. In this chase, Metis kept transforming into different creatures to try and get away. She eventually turned into a fly and Zeus ate her. This did not kill Metis, though, and she travelled through his body and made a home in his head, and it was here that she gave birth to Athena.

  • ☆ Athena began banging on the inside of Zeus’ head, trying to get out. This gave Zeus headaches that were so bad that he asked Hephaestus to cut his head open to find what was causing it.

  • ☆ Hephaestus did what was asked of him and after opening Zeus’ skull, Athena emerged fully grown and equipped with armour. Metis’ fate was never discussed again so we have to assume that she continued living inside Zeus’ head.

  • ☆ She was Zeus’ favourite child and he trusted her with his powerful thunderbolts.

  • ☆ She was also known as the Goddess of War and was a fierce protector of people and cities that worshipped her.

GOD OF WINE

  • ☆ His parents were Zeus and Semele, who was a human. This made Dionysus a demi-God and he was the only Olympian who had a mortal mother.

  • ☆ Once Semele was pregnant, Hera disguised herself as an old lady and told Semele that she should demand to see Zeus’ true form. When interacting with mortals, Gods had to disguise their true being because humans would die if they saw it. Zeus, who was smitten with Semele, granted her one wish and she wished to see Zeus, as he really was. Because he had granted her a wish, Zeus could not deny her of this, and Semele died.

  • ☆ Zeus managed to rescue his unborn son by taking him from Semele’s womb and sewing him into his thigh. A few months later, Dionysus was born from the wound Zeus cut into his thigh and he was taken to be raised by rain nymphs, where he was safe from Hera.

  • ☆ He was the first to turn grapes into wine and many festivals were held for him, where wine was drunk and music was played.

  • ☆ He was the last God to join Olympus and Hestia stood down to allow him to take her place.

  • ☆ His parents were Zeus and Maia and, surprisingly for a child of one of Zeus' affairs, there are no stories of Hera trying to sabotage him.

  • ☆ He was an extraordinary baby. At less than 1 day old, he pulled himself out of his crib and announced to his mother that he was going on a walk. On this walk, he found a field full of cows, which he led back to the cave that he had left his mother in.

  • ☆ What he didn’t know, however, was that these cows belonged to Apollo and were very important to him. Apollo followed the cows' prints to Hermes and was ready to punish the thief.

  • ☆ In the time it took Apollo to reach him, Hermes had invented the lyre, which is a small stringed instrument. He offered this to Apollo as an apology and returned all but 2 of the cows to him, one of which had been eaten by him and his mother and the other sacrificed to the Gods of Olympus. Apollo was very impressed by Hermes and the lyre and took the child to Olympus to meet his family, who were all very fond of him.

  • ☆ He acted as a messenger for the Gods and was considered a ‘trickster’ because he was so clever and cunning.

  • ☆ He was known for his winged sandals and winged helmet which allowed him to fly, making him perfect for the role of messenger.
